# Changelog

All notable changes to Jamais Vu are listed here. Newest first.

## 1.9.65 — First public release

The system as it stands after a long private campaign. Everything below has been played at a real table, not just written.

**The sheet**

- Detective and NPC sheets: 24 skills across 4 attributes, morale, health, XP, interjection tokens, réal
- Base value, modifiers from gear and thoughts, and the rolled total kept separate
- Inventory: weapons, armour, clothing, tools, drugs — equipped items feed their modifier into the relevant skill
- Thought Cabinet: thoughts cook over time, penalising their voice-skill until they ripen into a bonus
- Quests with stages and item triggers that advance them automatically
- Seven worldviews, each with two bonuses and a cost

**Checks**

- 2d6 + skill against a difficulty, white / yellow / red
- Snake eyes always fail, double sixes always succeed
- Check registry: what's passed stays passed, what's locked stays locked, what can reopen reopens as the skill grows

**Running a case**

- The Loop — a doom clock filling on failed checks and passing hours, throwing incidents at its thresholds
- Six day phases, each able to fire a matching Foundry playlist
- Investigation board with nodes and threads, revealed to players card by card
- Case journal that fills itself as things come to light
- City map with markers on the Foundry canvas, dossiers behind each pin
- Locations and scenes as actors, with hooks, leads and quick travel

**Tools**

- Oracle: yes/no, twists, names, places, case sparks
- NPC generator: estate, role, politics, manner of speech, starting condition
- Skill voices posted to chat as interjections
- Factions and reputation across five estates
- Shops, containers, Item Piles support
- 57 conditions with modifiers, durations and icons
- Light combat — no initiative grind
- GM console built for two game masters at once, with a shared scene and a text dispenser
- Full-size chat window, speaking queue, case export and import

**Character creation**

- The "Awakening": a narrative text quest that builds skills, worldview and appearance, and hands back a finished sheet with a portrait prompt

**Localisation**

- Eight interface languages: EN, RU, UK, CS, DE, FR, ES, PL — 942 keys
- Each player picks their own language; it's remembered in their browser
- Some long-form text falls back to English outside EN and RU

**Compendiums**

- 120 pieces of stock gear, 19 thoughts, 1 scene

**Compatibility**

- Requires Foundry VTT v12 or newer. Verified on v14.
